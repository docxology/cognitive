# Illusory Contours

Illusory contours are perceived edges or boundaries that do not physically exist in the visual stimulus. They represent a fundamental aspect of perceptual organization and demonstrate the constructive nature of visual perception.

## Core Characteristics

### Basic Properties
1. **Perceptual Features**
   - Subjective contours
   - Boundary completion
   - Surface perception
   - Figure-ground organization

2. **Stimulus Conditions**
   - Inducing elements
   - Spatial arrangement
   - Contrast requirements
   - Temporal factors

### Types of Illusions
1. **Kanizsa Figures**
   - Triangle
   - Square
   - Circle
   - Complex shapes

2. **Other Configurations**
   - Ehrenstein illusion
   - Abutting gratings
   - Line-end induced
   - Texture-defined
